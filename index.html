The Factories

The most common factory air pollutants are greenhouse gases from the burning of fossil fuels. Factories contribute to water and land pollution by acidifying rain, chemical spills and disposal of toxic waste.
Factories use fossil fuels to produce the products, these fossil fuels produce carbon dioxide and sulfur dioxide which increase the amount of greenhouse gases in the air. Factories also cause harm to the natural environment, the waste from the factories make the nutrients in the water that was already present at abnormal rates. This causes death of marine life. The ozone layer is also harmed as a result of waste from factories, the ozone layer protects the world from ultraviolet rays, however the ozone layer is getting closer to the ground due to built up greenhouse gases which becomes harmful to human tissue, and crops.
